Heat & mobility playbook

  • Midday is A/C time. Early September runs ~90°F with 75–79% humidity; it eases late-month. Do outdoor stops before ~11 AM or after ~5 PM (evenings dip to a pleasant ~72°F) and stay indoors 11–3.
  • Let cars & red streetcars do the walking. Take door-to-door rideshares. The red streetcar lines (Canal, Riverfront, Rampart–St. Claude) are wheelchair accessible; the historic St. Charles green line is not — high step, no handrail — so rideshare to the Garden District instead.
  • Borrow wheels & seats. The WWII Museum and the Aquarium rent wheelchairs and have seating everywhere — use them on hot days.
  • Pack: refillable water, a cooling towel, a personal misting fan, wide-brim hats, sunscreen, and one umbrella (sun and pop-up storms). Alternate every cocktail with water.
  • Reset points: a hotel-pool break and a snoball keep the whole crew (and the 7-year-old) from melting.

❄️ Stay cool

The whole trip is engineered around this — here's the toolkit.

Snoball stands
Hansen's Sno-Bliz (Uptown, an 85-year legend), Plum Street, and year-round Pandora's — feather-fine shaved ice, the fastest core-temp drop in the city.
🧒🚗
A/C escapes
Shops at Canal Place (upscale mall + cinema, Quarter edge) and the Riverwalk outlets (CBD riverfront) — cool, seated, easy browsing between sights.
❄️🪑🚋
City Park misting stations
Public misting machines and cold-water fountains under the giant shady oaks — a genuine relief on a hot morning.
🌳🧒
Rhythm of the day
Outdoors early & after 5 PM, A/C 11–3, a midday hotel-pool reset. Light layers — indoor A/C runs cold.

🚋 Get around (no car)

Rideshare for door-to-door comfort; red streetcars for the flat, breezy, accessible routes.

Rideshare (Uber / Lyft)
Everywhere, and the kindest option for the heat & low-walk adult. Airport (MSY) → downtown runs ~25–40 min / ~$35–40.
🚗🪑
Streetcar fares
$1.25 a ride, or a Jazzy Pass — $3 day / $8 three-day / $15 week — in the RTA "Le Pass" app.
🚋
♿ Accessible lines only
Ride the red cars — Canal, Riverfront, Rampart–St. Claude. Skip the historic St. Charles green line (high step, no handrail); rideshare to the Garden District instead.
🚋🪑
Cluster by neighborhood
French Quarter/CBD (aquarium, museums, beignets, Sazerac Bar) · City Park/Mid-City (Children's Museum, Singing Oak, Parkway) · Uptown/Garden District (snoballs, Commander's, Magazine). Minimizes hops.
🚗
Good to know. Hours and prices shift — the swamp tours, Steamboat Natchez, WWII 4D film, Commander's Palace, and Preservation Hall all sell out, so book a day or two ahead. Your dates dodge the crowds: no festival lands that week and the Saints' first September home game isn't until Sep 27. It's hurricane season, so keep an eye on the forecast and stay flexible.
