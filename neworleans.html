🧭 Quick decisions — tap your situation, get a plan

Everything here is fully indoors.

  • National WWII Museum — hours of A/C, elevators & the 4D film →
  • Audubon Aquarium & Insectarium — sharks, penguins & butterflies under one roof →
  • JamNOLA — 29 interactive art rooms, a 10-min ride to the Marigny →
  • Mardi Gras World — the working float-warehouse tour on the river →
  • Cabildo, Presbytère or the Pharmacy Museum — quick, cheap, delightfully weird →
  • Warm rain? The hotel pool still counts.

Beat the 11–3 peak; go outside early or after 5.

  • Sazerac House (free), Backstreet or the Pharmacy Museum — cool, compact, A/C →
  • Hotel pool reset + a snoball at Hansen's — the whole crew recovers →
  • Steamboat Natchez — a breezy river ride with an A/C dining room →
  • A red streetcar loop — windows-open breeze, and the ride is the activity →

Stay close, walk little.

  • Café du Monde beignets + Napoleon House Pimm's — both a short flat stroll in the Quarter →
  • Hotel pool, then an easy Creole dinner — Gumbo Shop or Muriel's on the square →
  • A short Riverfront (red) streetcar loop — sit, roll, people-watch →

Sugar, shade, water — in that order.

  • A snoball (Hansen's), District Donuts, or the Roman Candy cart — fast sugar, fast fix →
  • The hotel pool — always the hotel pool →
  • The Aquarium touch pool or City Park's Storyland — a full reset →

Cooler air, live music.

  • Preservation Hall early "Big Shot" show — book ahead — it sells out →
  • The Jazz Museum set or a Frenchmen St stroll — real jazz, kid-OK early →
  • A spin through JamNOLA — lights + music, indoors →

Your 6:10 PM flight leaves a full final day.

  • Beignets + the French Market — one more slow Quarter morning →
  • Easy brunch — Stanley or Ruby Slipper — + a last Magazine St stop →
  • Bag storage at the hotel after checkout — then head to MSY ~3:30 PM →

Quarter wins within a few flat blocks.

  • Café du Monde + a Jackson Square wander — beignets, buskers & the cathedral →
  • The Pharmacy Museum or the Cabildo — quick, cheap, A/C & delightfully odd →
  • A courtyard drink at Napoleon House — 1797 vibe, Pimm's Cup, shade →
  • Pralines poured at Aunt Sally's — watch, taste, done in 20 min →

No booking? These take walk-ins.

  • Central Grocery muffuletta — split one, picnic on the river →
  • Gumbo Shop or Killer PoBoys — easy, fast, kid-safe →
  • Felix's — oysters & a spicy Bloody Mary — raw, chargrilled, all-ages →
  • Mother's — the Ferdi po'boy — cafeteria line, no reservation →

Let the 7-year-old burn it off.

  • The hotel pool — always the first answer →
  • City Park — Storyland & the carousel — playground + antique rides, Canal streetcar →
  • Aquarium touch pool — hands-on stingrays, all A/C →
  • Crescent Park or the Roman Candy cart — open space + a taffy chase →

A real drink with the kid in tow.

  • Napoleon House or a hotel courtyard bar — shaded, seated, relaxed →
  • A Molly's go-cup, strolled — frozen Irish coffee to walk with →
  • The best Bloody Marys (feeling spicy) — Felix's, Molly's, Maison →

Take home the good stuff.

  • The French Market — pralines, hot sauce, masks, crafts →
  • Magazine St — 6 miles of local shops — books, art, kids' finds →
  • Faulkner House Books / FQ galleries — a quiet browse off the square →

Best light, coolest air, no crowds.

  • Crescent Park or the riverfront at dawn — skyline + river, flat & empty →

🎠 Just for kids

The 7-year-old's greatest hits — the kid magnets, tagged for heat & walking.

🧒
Louisiana Children's Museum · City Park
30,000 sq ft of hands-on: a climbing wall, build-your-own Creole cottage, and the world through a honeybee's eyes — cool and indoor.
❄️🪑🧒
🧒
Carousel Gardens · City Park
The 1906 "flying horses" carousel, a ladybug roller coaster, and a little train that loops the park. In fall it runs Fri–Sun ~11–6 (Storyland is open Tue–Sun) — confirm your day before going.
🧒☀️
🧒
Storyland · City Park
A fairy-tale playground of climb-on storybook sets under shady oaks — walk in from Carousel Gardens.
🧒🌳
🧒

Sunday's headliner — hold a small alligator from the shaded pontoon. (Already in the day plan.)
🧒🌳🪑
🧒
Audubon Zoo touch pool · Uptown
A 60-ft touch pool of sharks & stingrays, plus a "Sky Tykes" ropes course sized for ages 2–7. Go at opening — it's outdoors and bakes by midday.
🧒☀️🚗
🧒
Try on a crown · Mardi Gras World
Watch giant floats get built and try on Carnival costumes — indoor, A/C, guided. (Also in the day plan.)
❄️🧒

36 shady holes of New-Orleans-themed mini golf across two full courses.

Algiers Point ferry · from the Aquarium · $2
A 5-minute Mississippi crossing with skyline views — cheap, seated, and a real thrill for a kid. Wander historic Algiers Point + Confetti Park playground on the far side.
🧒🪑🌳
🧒
Crescent Park · Bywater riverfront
Climb the "rusty rainbow" bridge for the best skyline view and a flat riverside stroll — quiet and open, just past the French Market.
🧒☀️
🧒
French QuarTour Kids · French Quarter · guided
A kid-led Pirate Treasure Hunt (ages 4–7, with dress-up) or a gentle Ghost Hunt — all within a 6-block Quarter radius, so low-walking and right by your base.
🧒🌳🪑
🧒
Kids' cooking class · NO School of Cooking · FQ
Hands-on in a historic Quarter kitchen — the kiddo helps make gumbo, jambalaya and beignets, then eats the results. Indoor and cool.
🧒❄️🪑
🧒
Streetcar joyride · red lines · $1.25
Sometimes the ride is the activity — the clatter and wooden seats are a hit. Take the red Riverfront or Canal line for an easy loop.
🧒🚋🪑
🧒
Cascade Stables pony rides · Audubon Park
Pony rides and gentle trail rides for ages 4+ under the Uptown oaks — pair it with the zoo, which is right there.
🧒☀️🚗
🧒
The Fly + Audubon playgrounds · Uptown riverside
Three playgrounds, big oak-shaded lawns, and a riverfront to run off energy — the locals' spot to let a kid loose behind the zoo.
🧒🌳🚗
🧒
City Park paddle boats · Big Lake
Rent a paddleboat or kayak on Big Lake, then ride the antique carousel and grab beignets nearby.
🧒☀️🚗
🧒
Mardi Gras Museum of Costumes · N Rampart · FQ edge
A dress-up costume closet — the kid suits up as Mardi Gras royalty amid king & queen regalia and Mardi Gras Indian suits. Indoor, cool, walkable (Thu–Mon, 12–4).
🧒❄️🪑
🧒
Cool Zoo splash park · Audubon Zoo
A splash pad with a lazy river tucked inside the zoo — the best way to cool a 7-year-old off in the heat. Pack a swimsuit.
🧒☀️🚗
🧒
"Bug Appétit" · Insectarium, FQ
Dare the kid to taste a chef-made bug bite (chocolate "chirp" cookies?) at the Insectarium — included with the Aquarium ticket. Indoor & A/C.
🧒❄️🪑
🧒
The Singing Oak · City Park
A giant live oak hung with wind chimes tuned to a pentatonic scale — it literally sings in the breeze. Free, magical, and steps from Storyland.
